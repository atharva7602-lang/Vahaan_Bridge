What is PUC?

Pollution Under
Control Certificate

A mandatory certificate under the Motor Vehicles Act confirming your vehicle's emissions are within permissible limits under India's BS-VI norms.

🌿

Mandatory by Law

Every vehicle in India must have a valid PUC certificate under Section 190 of the MV Act. Driving without it is a cognizable offence.

📱

Instant Digital Download

Download your PUC certificate instantly via VahanBridge. Accepted by all traffic police as valid proof under CMV Rule 139.

⚠️

₹10,000 Penalty

Driving without a valid PUC attracts a fine of ₹10,000 and/or imprisonment up to 6 months under Sec. 190(2) MV Act 2019.

🔍

Real-time Verification

Traffic authorities, insurers and RTOs can instantly verify your PUC certificate via VahanBridge — linked to the national VAHAN database.
